Anzac Day, observed on April 25th each year, is a significant national occasion in both Australia and New Zealand. It marks the anniversary of the first major military action fought by Australian and New Zealand forces during World War I, at Gallipoli in 1915. The day honors the courage, sacrifice, and comradeship of the Anzacs—Australian and New Zealand Army Corps—as well as all those who have served and died in wars, conflicts, and peacekeeping operations. Anzac Day is a time for reflection and remembrance, bringing communities together to commemorate the values of bravery and unity that the Anzacs exemplified.
